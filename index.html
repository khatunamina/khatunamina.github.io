📄1. Exploring Biomarkers and Pathways in Preeclampsia and Cardiovascular Diseases: A Bioinformatics and Machine Learning Approach

Authors:Mst. Amina Khatun; Md. Faruk Hosen; Mohammad Sarwar Hossain Mollah; Muhammad Shahin Uddin; Monir Morshed

Conference Location: Kushtia, Bangladesh

Preeclampsia (PE) is a life-threatening obstetric disorder that can lead to significant gestational and fetal morbidity and mortality. Recent studies indicate that individuals with a history of PE are at an elevated risk of developing cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) later in life. Despite the clinical association between PE and CVDs, the shared molecular mechanisms and regulatory genes underlying these conditions remain insufficiently characterized. Through this study, we focused on elucidate the concordant molecular pathways and endowment therapeutic targets implicated in both PE and CVDs to enhance early detection and intervention strategies. An integrative systems biology framework, combined with machine learning techniques, was applied to determine key Differentially expressed genes (DEGs) from transcriptomic data. Functional enrichment was conducted using Gene Ontology (GO) and pathway analysis to uncover biological processes and signaling cascades. Hub genes were identified by building protein–protein interaction (PPI) networks. Additionally, transcription factor (TF)-gene and microRNA (miRNA)-gene regulatory networks were explored to highlight upstream regulatory elements. Finally, computational drug analysis revealed potential therapeutic compounds targeting both conditions, which may function as dual-purpose agents for treatment and serve as prognostic biomarkers for cardiovascular risk assessment in women previously diagnosed with preeclampsia.

🔗 https://doi.org/10.1109/COMPAS67506.2025.11381783

📄2.Leveraging Machine Learning to Uncover Molecular Pathways and Candidate Drugs in Ovarian Cancer and Polycystic Ovary Syndrome

Authors:Francis Rudra D Cruze; Amina Khatun; Md. Faruk Hosen; Mohammad Sarwar Hossain Mollah; Md Nasimul Kader; Muhammad Shahin Uddin

Conference Location: Chittagong, Bangladesh

Ovarian cancer (OC) and polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) significantly impact the lives of millions of women worldwide. PCOS affects 8-13% of women of reproductive age, while OC is regarded as the deadliest form of gynecological cancer. Their impacts are enormous, yet the molecular links between these two diseases are not well defined. This study tries to explore this important knowledge gap using an integrated bioinformatics approach to unravel common genetic signatures and potential therapeutic candidates for PCOS and OC. We used differential gene expression (DEGs) analysis, protein-protein interaction (PPI) networks, pathway enrichment analysis, and regulatory network analysis on two microarray datasets. Utilizing the sophisticated machine learning approach known as minimum redundancy maximum relevance (mRMR), we detected 24 genes that exhibited differential expression across both conditions. Analysis of the PPI network highlighted ten prominent hub genes - GIGYF2, FGB, ZEB2, S100P, TNFRSF10C, CP, LMAN2, RAPH1, UBC, and KDM4D - which are presumably integral to the molecular interactions between PCOS and OC. Functional enrichment analysis identified significant biological pathways; the regulatory network analysis revealed complex TFs, miRNAs, and DEGs interactions. More importantly, we have predicted potential therapeutic compounds apigenin and sirolimus targeting the shared DEGs. These results provide a crucial clue toward the possible molecular underlying mechanisms of PCOS and OC for further therapeutic and diagnostic interventions. The present study is epitomizing the effectiveness of integrating different bioinformatic approaches to uncover complex disease mechanisms and guide further investigational efforts in the field of women's health.

https://doi.org/10.1109/ECCE64574.2025.11013839

📄3. A Machine Learning and Bioinformatics Approach for Identifying Key Biomarkers in Bipolar Disorder and Schizophrenia

Authors: Mst. Mostary Khatun, Tanvir Hasan,Mst. Amina Khatun,Md. Faruk Hosen,Monir Morshed

Venue: 11th IEEE International Women in Engineering (WIE) Conference on Electrical and Computer Engineering 2026 (IEEE WIECON-ECE 2026) at Long Beach Hotel, Cox’s Bazar, Bangladesh.

Bipolar disorder (BD) and schizophrenia (SCZ) are serious psychiatric conditions that share similar clinical charac teristics and genetic predispositions. Nevertheless, the fundamen tal molecular mechanisms linking these disorders have not been fully explored. This research seeks to clarify the common molec ular pathways and potential therapeutic targets in BD and SCZ by employing an integrated bioinformatics and machine learning methodology. Gene expression datasets were sourced from public databases, and an analysis of differential gene expression was performed using the Limma package. A feature selection method based on filtering, specifically Fisher Score, was utilized to pinpoint the most significant genes common to both disorders. We detected 30 shared differentially expressed genes (DEGs) in BD and SCZ. Functional enrichment analysis through Gene Ontology (GO) demonstrated a significant connection to mitochondrial ac tivity and cellular respiration. KEGG pathway analysis addition ally indicated links to oxidative phosphorylation and pathways associated with neurodegenerative diseases. The development and clustering of the protein–protein interaction (PPI) network revealed five pivotal hub genes—NDUFS2, NDUFB2, UQCRQ, COX7C, and NDUFA13—all of which are integral components of the mitochondrial respiratory chain, implying a shared metabolic dysfunction in both conditions. Furthermore, regulatory network analysis revealed complex interactions involving transcription factors (TFs), microRNAs (miRNAs), and the common DEGs, highlighting intricate gene regulatory mechanisms. Predictive drug analysis pinpointed potential therapeutic agents aimed at these common molecular signatures. In summary, our results offer new insights into the shared biological foundations of BD and SCZ, providing encouraging pathways for future diagnostic and therapeutic approaches.

📄4. Enhanced Prediction of Interleukin-13 Inducing Peptides Using Sequence Embedding and CNN Classifier

Authors:M. A. Murad, Sanjida Akter, Mst. Amina Khatun, Md. Faruk Hosen, and Md Nasimul Kader

Venue: 11th IEEE International Women in Engineering (WIE) Conference on Electrical and Computer Engineering 2026 (IEEE WIECON-ECE 2026) at Long Beach Hotel, Cox’s Bazar, Bangladesh.

Interleukin-13 (IL-13), a cytokine produced by im mune cells, is crucial in allergic disease pathogenesis, contributing to conditions such as asthma with effects on airway hyper responsiveness, mucus production and obstruction.It’s linked to suppressing immune surveillance, potentially aiding cancer development. The ability to predict IL-13 inducing peptides is crucial for safe design of protein-based therapeutics, especially in immuno therapy field.However, Experimental identification is time-consuming and costly, so it’s necessary to construct effective predictors. This paper introduces a novel predictor called IL-13-DeepEmbed, which is developed to accurately predict IL-13 inducing peptides from sequences employing a convolution neural network (CNN) architecture. Firstly, we perform Word2Vec, GloVe and fastText feature extraction meth ods. Applying the FastText dataset, CNN attains a maximum AUC value of 0.6228.Secondly, The Adaptive Synthetic Sam pling Approach(ADASYN) is applied in fasttext unbalanced datasets.Then, SHapley Additive exPlanations(SHAP) is applied to eliminate unnecessary and inconsistent features. Finally, the significant features are fed into the CNN classifier to build the IL-13-DeepEmbed architecture, resulting in 0.9649 as AUC value. The result demonstrates, IL-13-DeepEmbed surpasses traditional methods in inducing IL-13, providing novel insights for identifying IL-13-inducing peptides.

📄5.Gastric Cancer Classification Using a Hybrid CNN-Attention-LSTM Model with Deep Feature Extraction.

Authors:Md. Faruk Hosen, Mst. Amina Khatun,Md. Suhag Ali,Md. Shakil Ahmed,Sazia Sharmin,Muhammad Shahin Uddin

Venue: 2026 IEEE 2nd International Conference on Quantum Photonics, Artificial Intelligence, and Networking (QPAIN) 16– 18 April 2026, Chittagong, Bangladesh

Gastric cancer (GC) ranks as the fifth most preva lent malignancy worldwide, and its early detection is crucial for reducing mortality. The current clinical gold standard re lies on histopathological image examination, which despite its diagnostic reliability remains manual, labor-intensive, and time consuming. Consequently, there has been increasing interest in computer-aided diagnostic (CAD) systems to assist pathol ogists in decision-making. Although deep learning approaches have demonstrated considerable potential, conventional models often capture only a restricted set of discriminative image features, thereby limiting classification performance. To address these challenges, this study introduces a comprehensive com putational framework for automated GC classification using 245,196 histopathological images divided into two categories. The pipeline integrates data preprocessing with multiple filtering strategies, feature extraction, feature selection, and a CNN Attention-LSTM (CALNet) classifier. For feature extraction, three pre-trained CNN models were fine-tuned under diverse parameter configurations through transfer learning to obtain discriminative representations. Subsequently, Local Interpretable Model-Agnostic Explanations (LIME) was employed as a feature selection strategy to discard non-informative attributes and enhance generalization. Finally, the proposed CALNet model was utilized to ensure reliable and interpretable predictions. Our framework achieved an accuracy of 98.74%, AUC of 98.47%, specificity of 91.00%, precision of 98.15%, and MCC of 91.21%. Experimental outcomes demonstrate that the proposed approach substantially outperforms existing methodologies, underscoring its potential to serve as a clinical decision-support system for gastric cancer diagnosis.

📰XAI-AttBiLSTM: an explainable two-stage feature selection framework with Attention-based BiLSTM for gynecological cancer risk module identification

Authors: Mst. Mostary Khatun1 · Francis Rudra D Cruze2,3 · Mst. Amina Khatun4 · Md. Faruk Hosen3,4 Muhammad Shahin Uddin1 · Monir Morshed1

Journal:XAI-AttBiLSTM: an explainable two-stage feature selection framework with Attention-based BiLSTM for gynecological cancer risk module , Accepted: 17 January 2026

Publisher: Springer Nature 2026  |  Impact Factor: 2.0  |  Indexed: Scopus

Gynecological cancers (GC) rank among the most common cancers affecting women and are leading causes of cancer related morbidity and mortality worldwide. Despite notable clinical progress, effective early diagnostic biomarkers and tar geted therapeutic options are still lacking. In this study, we propose a comprehensive machine learning and bioinformatics framework to identify potential biomarkers and therapeutic compounds for GC. Initially, four gene expression microarray datasets were used to extract a preliminary set of differentially expressed genes (DEGs). We employed a two-step feature selection approach: first, Boruta was applied, followed by LIME as the final feature selection technique. The final gene subset was then fed into our Attention-based Bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory (AttBiLSTM) model, which demon strated better predictive performance compared to other classifiers. Thirty common genes, resulting from the intersection of DEGs and the gene subset derived from machine learning, were identified. Gene Ontology (GO) and KEGG pathway enrichment analysis revealed enrichment in biological processes associated with GC. The protein-protein interactions (PPI) network identified 10 hub genes: TK1, CRMP1, KIF5A, TUBB2A, METTL20, TSC2, RHEB, A2M, UBC, and RBM48. The transcription factor (TF) regulatory network added the TF-gene and TF-miRNA interactions and identified five core regulators that had regulatory network associations in the PPI. Molecular docking also identified that five of the candidate targets had strong binding affinity with four GC drugs. These findings indicate that the identified hub genes show potential as biomarkers for early detection and prognosis, while the associated compounds may serve as promising candidates for the therapeutic development of GC.

🔗 https://doi.org/10.1007/s13721-026-00739-x
